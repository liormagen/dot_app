Dot Story
20 Stories — App Format
Connect-the-dots image, subject word, and narration scripts ready for production.
How to read this document
Each story follows the same structure that the app already supports. The flow is: opening narration → first connect-the-dots → image reveals + subject word spoken → coloring → narration before next part → and so on.
In each story below you will find:
Companion character — the figure that appears on the story transition screen and grows or changes through the story.
Emotional arc — the feeling journey for the child.
Opening narration — plays before the first dots appear.
Per part: the connect-the-dots image (the picture revealed), the subject word (shown on screen and spoken aloud when the image is fully connected), and the narration that plays after that part is colored, before the next part.
Closing narration — plays on the story completion screen, with all images shown together.
Tone notes
All language is age-6-appropriate: short sentences, simple words, sound effects (whoosh, plop, drip) where they help. Each story has a small dip — a worry, a problem, a moment of feeling small — and then lifts the child back up to safety, joy, or wonder. Voiceover talent should read warmly, with energy on the action moments and softness on the feelings.
Color key in this document
🖼  Yellow blocks = the connect-the-dots image to be drawn.
"Green blocks = the spoken narration line. This is exactly what the voiceover artist will read."
